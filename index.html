Evidence & References
Incidence of first unprovoked seizure (commonly cited ~50–70 per 100,000/year):
  1. Neligan A. The epidemiology of epilepsy. Epilepsy Society. 2015. Reports first unprovoked seizure incidence typically 50–70 per 100,000/year in industrialised countries.
    https://epilepsysociety.org.uk/sites/default/files/2020-08/Chapter01Neligan-2015_0.pdf
  2. Rizvi S, Ladino LD, Hernandez-Ronquillo L, Téllez-Zenteno JF. Incidence and prevalence of epilepsy and unprovoked seizures. Seizure. 2017;49:13-18.
    https://www.seizure-journal.com/article/S1059-1311(17)30118-8/fulltext
Seizure mimics in first seizure clinics (informing uplift factors):
  1. UK first seizure clinic audits report approximately 30–50% of referrals are ultimately diagnosed as seizure mimics. Example:
    Seizure Journal (2023 audit)
  2. St George’s Hospital first seizure clinic audit: 41.7% mimics overall; syncope ~22%, functional/dissociative attacks ~6%, TIA ~1–2%.
    https://reference-global.com/download/article/10.21307/joepi-2017-0004.pdf
  3. NIHR Evidence alert summarising recurrence after a first unprovoked seizure (meta-analysis): 36% have a second seizure within 1 year.
    https://evidence.nihr.ac.uk/alert/epilepsy-what-are-the-chances-of-having-a-second-seizure/
  4. American Academy of Neurology / American Epilepsy Society practice guideline: SUDEP incidence in adults ~1.2 per 1,000 patient-years (systematic review).
    https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5486432/
  5. NICE evidence review notes overall SUDEP rate around 1 in 1,000 people with epilepsy per year.
    https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK591158/
Uplift percentages and SUDEP calculations are illustrative planning defaults and should be calibrated using local service data.
