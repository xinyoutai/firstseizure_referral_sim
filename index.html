Evidence & Sources

Incidence of first unprovoked seizure (commonly cited 50–70 per 100,000/year):
  • Neligan A. The epidemiology of epilepsy. Epilepsy Society (2015). Reports first unprovoked seizure incidence typically 50–70 per 100,000/year in industrialised countries.
  • Rizvi S et al. Incidence and prevalence of epilepsy and unprovoked seizures. Seizure (2017).
  • Population-based cohort reviews (e.g., PMC8776562) supporting similar range in high-income countries.
Proportion of seizure mimics in first seizure clinics:
  • UK first seizure clinic audits reporting approximately 30–50% of referrals ultimately diagnosed as seizure mimics.
  • St George’s Hospital first seizure clinic audit: 41.7% mimics; syncope ~22%, functional ~6%, TIA ~1–2%.
  • Recent audits in Seizure journal confirming substantial mimic burden in first seizure pathways.

Uplift percentages used in this simulation are illustrative defaults derived from published audit proportions and should be calibrated using local service data where available.
